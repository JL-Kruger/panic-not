🤖 The Creatures You Encounter There

Understanding AI tools, recognizing BS, and staying safe

What Are We Actually Looking At Here?

AI isn't magic. It isn't thinking. It's sophisticated pattern matching combined with probability calculations. Understanding this helps you recognize both its capabilities and its limitations.

🎲

Probability, Not Intelligence

AI predicts the most likely next word based on patterns in training data. It doesn't "understand" meaning—it matches patterns.

📚

Training Data Matters

AI is only as good as what it learned from. Biased data = biased AI. Old data = outdated information.

🎭

Scaffolding Shapes Behavior

Hidden instructions (system prompts) determine personality and responses. Same AI, different instructions = completely different behavior.

AI Jargon Decoder

Bullshit vs. Actually Useful Terms

Marketing Hype With No Substance

When you hear these, ask "What does that actually do?"

"AI-Powered"

What they want you to think: Revolutionary intelligence!
Reality: Basic if/then statement or someone else's API
Ask: "What specifically is the AI doing that couldn't be done before?"

"Military-Grade Encryption"

What they want you to think: Unbreakable government security!
Reality: Standard AES-256 that everyone uses
Reality check: "Military-grade" is marketing fluff

"Sentient AI" / "Conscious AI"

What they want you to think: Has feelings and self-awareness!
Reality: Really convincing at seeming human-like
Truth: We can't even define consciousness in humans

"AGI" (when claiming current systems)

What they want you to think: Human-level AI achieved!
Reality: We don't have AGI. Anyone claiming we do is selling something
Fact: AGI doesn't exist yet (as of 2025)

Terms That Help You Understand

LLM (Large Language Model)

AI trained on massive text datasets to predict next words based on patterns
Why useful: Helps you understand these are probability machines, not thinking beings
Example: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini predict what words should come next

Token / Tokenization

Breaking text into chunks (often parts of words) that AI processes
Why useful: Explains weird word cuts and input limits
Example: "Unbelievable" = 3 tokens ("Un", "believ", "able")

Hallucination

When AI confidently generates false information
Why useful: Explains why you can't trust AI for facts without verification
Example: AI generates fake legal citations that sound real but don't exist

Prompt / System Prompt

Instructions you give (prompt) and hidden developer instructions (system prompt)
Why useful: Helps you get better results and recognize designed behavior
Example: "You are a helpful assistant" makes ChatGPT apologize constantly

Training Data Cutoff

AI only "knows" information from before training completion date
Why useful: Explains why AI is confidently wrong about recent events
Example: Model trained in 2023 doesn't know about 2024 events

Bias (in AI)

Systematic unfairness caused by biased training data or reward systems
Why useful: Explains discrimination even without intentional programming
Example: Hiring AI replicates historical discrimination patterns

Going Deeper

Context Window

How much text (in tokens) AI can "remember" in one conversation
Explains why AI forgets early parts of long conversations

Temperature

Setting controlling randomness (low = predictable, high = creative)
Explains why same prompt sometimes gives different answers

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

AI looks up info from database before generating response
Reduces hallucinations by grounding in actual data

Red Teaming

Deliberately trying to break/fool AI to find vulnerabilities
Critical safety practice before public release

📸 Image Deepfakes

"Nudify" apps: Take clothed photos and generate fake explicit versions. Primarily targeting teens through classmate harassment.
Documented cases: Lancaster County incident (20 high school students), elementary schools finding deepfake content on school tablets.
Response: Screenshot evidence, report to school/police immediately. Creating/distributing is illegal in many jurisdictions.

🎤 Voice Cloning

Emergency scams: Synthetic audio mimics family members to create false emergencies ("Mom, I'm in trouble, send money").
How it works: Scraped audio from social media videos, voicemails, or public recordings provides enough voice data.
Response: Establish family "safe word" for emergencies. Verify through different channel before acting.

🎬 Video Deepfakes

Manipulation at scale: Political misinformation, celebrity impersonation, reputation damage through fabricated video.
Current state: High-quality fakes require expertise, but quality improving rapidly. Face-swap apps increasingly accessible.
Response: Verify through multiple sources. Check for artifacts (weird eye movement, lighting inconsistencies, unnatural mouth movements).

Why Detection Is Hard

  • Arms race: As detection improves, generation improves faster
  • Context matters: Low-quality video can be real; high-quality can be fake
  • Confirmation bias: We believe what matches our expectations
  • Speed of spread: Fake content goes viral before verification

🛡️ Practical AI Safety

For Using AI Tools

✓ Never trust without verification: AI hallucinates confidently. Check facts independently.
✓ Understand limitations: Training data cutoff, bias, lack of reasoning.
✓ Don't share sensitive info: Assume anything you tell AI could be logged/leaked.
✓ Learn to prompt effectively: Clear instructions get better results.

For Protecting Against AI Threats

✓ Limit public content: Less available data = harder to deepfake you.
✓ Establish verification protocols: Family safe words, callback numbers.
✓ Question everything: "Too perfect" photos/audio deserve skepticism.
✓ Document and report: Evidence helps prosecution and protects others.

For Teaching Kids About AI

✓ Demystify: Show them how it works (these demos!) to remove "magic."
✓ Critical thinking: "How could this be wrong? What's the source?"
✓ Ethical use: Just because you can generate something doesn't mean you should.
✓ Recognize deepfakes: Practice spotting artifacts together.

🤔 Evaluating AI Claims

Your BS detector toolkit

When someone says "Our AI can..."

1
What does it actually do?

"AI-powered" means nothing. Demand specifics.

2
What's the training data?

Biases in data = biases in output. Public? Proprietary? How recent?

3
What are the limitations?

Any claim without limitations is lying. All AI has limits.

4
How is it being evaluated?

What metrics? Tested on what data? By whom?

5
Who's responsible when it fails?

If no clear answer, that's a red flag.

🚩 Warning Signs of Snake Oil

✗ Vague technical claims
✗ No mention of limitations
✗ "Solves everything" promises
✗ Buzzword stacking without explanation
✗ Claims of human-level intelligence
✗ "You wouldn't understand the technical details"

The Best Response to AI Jargon

The best response to confusing AI jargon isn't memorizing definitions—it's confidently saying "Explain that in normal words" until you get a real answer.

If they can't explain it simply, either they don't understand it themselves, or they're hoping you won't ask questions.
