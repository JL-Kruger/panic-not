üö¶ Traffic Light System

GREEN = Safe to Handle
  • Spam/scam attempts (not targeted)
  • Accidentally seeing inappropriate content
  • Minor privacy mistakes
  • Technical questions
‚Üí Discuss with family, learn together
AMBER = Needs Adult Help
  • Persistent harassment or bullying
  • Fake accounts impersonating someone
  • Pressure from online contacts
  • Uncomfortable requests
‚Üí Tell trusted adult immediately
RED = Call Authorities
  • Sexual content involving minors
  • Threats of violence or harm
  • Blackmail or extortion
  • Adult solicitation of minors
‚Üí Document, report to police/Childline

üìã Response Protocol Checklist

1
STOP

Don't delete anything. Don't respond to threats. Take a breath.

2
DOCUMENT

Screenshot everything. Save URLs, usernames, timestamps. Evidence matters.

3
ASSESS

Green, Amber, or Red? Determines next action.

4
ACT

Report to appropriate channel: family discussion, platform report, or authorities.

5
SUPPORT

Check in on emotional wellbeing. Follow up as needed. This is not their fault.

üéØ Final Takeaways

01

Awareness, Not Panic

The threats are real, but manageable. Knowledge reduces fear and builds competence.

02

Patterns Over Perfection

You don't need to be an expert. Recognizing common attack patterns is enough.

03

Communication Over Control

Trust-based relationships where kids report problems work better than surveillance.

04

Competence, Not Compliance

The goal is graduating digitally literate humans who make good decisions independently.

05

Habits Over Heroics

Small consistent security practices beat occasional extreme measures.

06

Questions Over Answers

Teaching kids to ask "Why does this need that?" is more valuable than memorizing rules.

Don't let this be a one-time thing

  • Do quarterly app audit (delete unused)
  • Practice "what would you do if..." scenarios
  • Update passwords (use password manager)
Ongoing
  • Make digital safety check-ins normal conversation
  • Stay current (threats evolve‚Äîyou should too)

Remember:

"Ukukhala akusuzi, ulwazi yilo olusizayo"

Crying does not help, it is knowledge that helps

You've taken the first step. The workshop might end, but the learning continues. Technology will keep changing. Threats will evolve. But the principles remain: awareness, critical thinking, communication, and competence.

You don't need to be perfect. You just need to be present, informed, and willing to learn alongside your kids.
